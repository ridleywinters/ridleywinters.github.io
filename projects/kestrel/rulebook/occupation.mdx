# Occupation

A character's **Occupation** is how the character "occupies their time."  The character's occupation defines what how the character will act when left to their own devices. Every occupation is coupled to a some form of motivation (whether it's to pursue money, fame, excellence at their craft, defense of what they don't want to lose, etc.) and a strategy to up hold that motivation. The occupation of a character could be, but is not necessarily, their profession.  Some example occupations might include:

* **Farmer**: motivated to make a profit raising crops
* **Builder**: motivated to construct building in exchange for money from others
* **Adventurer**: motivated to find treasure and defeat novel enemies
* **Alcoholic**: motivated, perhaps unconsciously, to find more to drink 
* **Vagabond**: motivated to wander

An occupation generally describes is how they spent their time, but each Occupation is coupled to a **Strategy** that defines what motivates the character in that activity and, therefore, how they approach it.



## Strategy

A single Occupation can have multiple possible Strategies.  Which Strategy a character chooses largely defines who they are. Let's take the example of the Farmer. Different Farmers might have different Strategies (and motiviations) in why they farm:

__Example: A family farmer__

A farmer, motiviated by the beauty of the field and to feed their family, has a strategy of cultivating small farms near their homes. They are not motivated to expand their fields once they have enough for their family, but instead will then try to improve the quality of what they are producing.  The success of this Strategy will likely be determined by the health of their family as well as the amount of time the character can spend with their family.


__Example: An ambitious business farmer__

A farmer, motivated to run a successful business, has a strategy of expanding their fields and increasing how much produce they can sell over time. They are motiviated by financial success which in term makes them measure the quality of what they grow.  

These varied strategies may lead to very different outcomes.  The different strategies also will have interesting effects when both are situated in the same area and other characters are looking to acquire the highest-quality food.

Ultimately the success of the strategy affects the character's [contentment](contentment), which is the primary health indicator of a character.

## Plans

A Strategy is a large, encompassing goal that drives the entire behavior of the character.  In order to make that Strategy happen, each strategy is composed of different smaller **Plans** that the character undertakes. 

These are series of tasks. If some of the tasks fail, the plan may adapt or adjust or cancel. These are more complex.  Plans are usually defined under the umbrella of particular Strategies (i.e. they are not reused across different strategies).

## Tasks

These are "atomic" things a character can do in the world. They either succeed or fail. It's like digging a block. Tilling the soil for farming. 

Tasks are "reusable".  Plans are composed as a sequence of Tasks.  Task can have simple options to allow variations. They also may have a small set of different outcomes.  Tasks can be Succeeded, Failed, Interrupted, or Canceled.  

A task may require certain Items or Skills to be undertaken. A Task can always be "forced" to be undertaken, but it will result in failure without the required Skills.  Items, Skills, and Conditions may also affect the probability of Success.

As Tasks are the building block of all Plans and Strategies, building a collection of high-quality Tasks ensure the world can support a robust set of behaviors.

Last a task may be composed of different *Steps*.  These are essentially sub-tasks which exist to help make it easier to develop new Plans in the gaming world.



## Future Directions

**Multiple occupations**.  Characters may have a profession as well as a hobby. They may have a daytime occupation as well as evening occupation.  They may have multiple occupations with rules about which one decides their actions.
